# Phase 4 — Pitch & Demo Readiness

**Prerequisite:** Phases 0–3. This phase makes it *feel like a product being built in the space*, not "I did this, I'm done."

**Goal:** The narrative and demo assets to stand in front of a panel or investor with confidence — showing what works, keeping the scaffolding backstage.

## Produce

- **The one-liner** — the chosen positioning (from Phase 2), in the product's honest voice.
- **Problem-worth-solving case** — why this matters now, for whom, grounded (Nigerian SME AP/AR pain, vendor-fraud/wrong-account risk, control gaps). Cite sources per org policy.
- **The hero demo script** — a ~60-second-to-3-minute walkthrough of the sharpened spine, step by step, on the real deployed app. Use the built-in demo affordances (seeded KYB NUBANs, `EXC-` failure prefix, 80/20 PSP sim). Name each screen and the point it proves.
- **What to show vs. keep backstage** — controls that demo well (four-eyes, PIN, KYB match, compliance gate, audit log) vs. scaffolding to leave out (simulated PSP internals, dev secrets, SQLite, known-limitation items).
- **Panel Q&A prep** — the hard questions (why not hold funds? what's real vs simulated? regulatory posture? what's the wedge? what breaks at scale?) with crisp, honest answers.
- **Credibility gaps to close before pitching** — anything from Phase 1 that would undermine the story if a reviewer clicked the wrong thing.

## Discipline

- Honest framing only. Do not claim simulated components are live. Distinguish built / simulated / roadmap plainly — reviewers reward that.
- Never surface secrets, PII, or customer data in demo assets.
